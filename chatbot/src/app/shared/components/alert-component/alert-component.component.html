Story

After a 25-year relationship, a 60-year-old woman named Jane faced the breakdown of her relationship when she discovered her partner had another relationship. As a result, she had to learn to be independent and support herself financially by getting a job. However, she also had to face several risks, including the impact on her mental health, the worry of being negatively judged, selling her house and buying new property, and the risk of her ex-partner clearing out savings accounts. Additionally, her children had access to her passwords and money, and they had taken the side of her ex-partner. To overcome these challenges, the woman took several actions. She had a meeting with the bank for advice and froze her bank and savings accounts. She kept records of all conversations with dates and times and took screenshots of everything for proof. She sought help from charities such as CAB (Citizen advice) and Age UK, ensured not to share passwords or changed them if necessary, and met with the bank to protect and support her.
